About me

I received my B.Eng. degree (2015) and Ph.D. degree (2019) in the Department of Information Engineering at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, advised by Sidharth Jaggi and Mayank Bakshi. From 2019-2022, I was a research fellow at National University of Singapore (with Vincent Y. F. Tan) and a researcher at Huawei Theory Lab. I was also fortunate to visit Matthieu Bloch at Georgia Tech in 2018.

My research interests lie broadly in the span of information theory, machine learning, and statistics. Specifically, I am passionate about uncovering the mathematical foundations and developing novel algorithms for reinforcement learning and community detection (a.k.a. clustering). My goal is to design efficient and effective solutions that are both theoretically sound and also have practical applications in real-world scenarios.
